When you do decide to meet the doctor with your child for the first time after you notice something different, it would be better if you prepare yourself. Keep some things that might help you explain the problem much more easily. I know it helped me, in convincing the doctor that we really have a concern and that we need help.

 Do not forget to carry “the booklet” that the maternity hospital provides you when you delivered your baby.
o Check for head circumference
 o Length of the baby at birth
 o Gestation period (how many weeks when you delivered)
 o Delivery: normal or caesarian

  Keeping a notepad/dairy of events/incidents that you might have observed
 Photographs in chronological order
 Videos of your child when he/she was (in your opinion) doing things normally and when things started changing (in chronological order)
 Any earlier medical records (if you consulted another doctor earlier)
